The Problem

AI is developing too rapidly for humanity to stay in control.

AI could be the best thing that ever happens to humanity. It could also be the last. A handful of companies are locked in an arms race, building the most powerful technology in human history as fast as they possibly can, with no oversight, no democratic accountability, and, by their own admission, without fully understanding what they're building.

How many jobs have to vanish?
How much of the internet has to be fake?
How many creators have to be buried under AI slop?
How many artists have to compete with clones of their own voice?
How many scams and deepfakes? How many elections undermined?
How many kids have to grow up shaped by algorithms no parent chose?
How close do we get to surveillance dystopias, AI-designed pandemics, killer robots?
Or, as experts keep warning, literal human extinction?

This situation is insane. And we all know it.

The Plan

Slow down. Give humanity time to figure out how to stay in control.

The root cause of all these dangers is the global AI arms race, which creates a race-to-the-bottom on safety and doesn't give humanity time to figure out how to regulate it effectively.

The root cause

Many regulations are harmful or unhelpful. We need humanity's best and brightest working on this, but it could take years to get the regulations right.

In TeamSeas, the goal was cleaning up beaches. Then we discovered most of the ocean's trash came from just three rivers. If we didn't address the root cause, the beaches would just get dirty again. Most proposed AI regulation is cleaning the beaches. The arms race is the river.

If you're driving 100 mph in fog, and you think there's a cliff somewhere ahead, you slow down. That's common sense. That's what we need to do with AI.

What we're demanding

1

An international agreement on AIBetween the US, China, and major nations: slow down, enforce real safety standards, and keep humans in control before it's too late.

2

Monitor the speedometerFrontier AI is built with specialized chips. Track and guard them, exactly like we track the ingredients for nukes and bioweapons. Governments set the speed limits on giant training runs.

3

An IAEA for AIThe International Atomic Energy Agency verifies nuclear compliance worldwide. We need the same watchdog for AI, with concrete treaty and "pause button" proposals already on the table.

This isn't a pipe dream. Hundreds of Nobel Prize winners, industry leaders, and heads of state are calling for something even more extreme: an outright ban on the development of ASI (Artificial Superintelligence).

Yes, we can slow down, and it would be easy compared to other problems humanity has solved. We can do this.

All four leading AI companies agree things may be going too fast. They say they would slow down if the others did. But none of them can stop unilaterally. That's what governments are for. And governments won't act without public pressure.

That's where we come in.

We've Done This Before

Humanity has stared down existential threats and won.

We limited nuclear weapons

You can't just buy the ingredients for nukes. They're tracked and guarded. We can track and guard the chips used to produce frontier AI the same way.

We saved the ozone layer

The world faced a hole in the sky. Through international cooperation, we fixed it.

We restricted thousands of dangerous technologies

Bans and restrictions humanity agreed on together. It's time to do it again.

Every time, it took international cooperation, and ordinary people who refused to be silent.
It's time to do it again.
